Start with Your Backyard Vision

When it comes to pool designs, there's no one-size-fits-all approach. The key is choosing a design that matches your aesthetic, your lifestyle, and of course, your budget. Here are a few designs to consider:

  • Infinity Pools: If you're aiming for a dramatic, visually stunning pool, an infinity pool might be just what you need. It creates the illusion that your pool water blends seamlessly into the horizon, perfect for homes with scenic views.
  • Natural Pools: For those who want something a little different, natural pools are a great option. These pools are designed to blend with the surrounding environment, often featuring stone accents and lush greenery.
  • Lap Pools: For fitness enthusiasts, a lap pool is ideal. Narrow and long, these pools are built for swimming laps, providing a sleek, minimalistic look without compromising on functionality.
  • Freeform Pools: If you want a pool that reflects the natural shape of your backyard, a freeform pool is the way to go. These pools are perfect for creating a more organic look, blending in with landscaping.
  • Pool and Spa Combos: Combine relaxation with functionality by having a pool and spa combo. Perfect for those who enjoy both swimming and soaking, these designs offer luxury and comfort.
  • Modern Pools: Clean lines, geometric shapes, and minimalist elements define modern pools. If you want a sleek, contemporary vibe, modern pool designs are a great choice.

Pool Materials to Consider

Choosing the right materials for your pool is just as important as the design itself. Some materials offer durability, while others focus on aesthetic appeal. Take a look at these material choices you can explore.

  • Concrete: Concrete pools are durable and versatile, allowing for various shapes and sizes. They are also customizable in terms of finishes and textures.
  • Fiberglass: Known for being low-maintenance and quick to install, fiberglass pools come in a range of pre-designed shapes and sizes.
  • Vinyl Liner: This is a more affordable option, offering smooth surfaces and easy installation, though it requires more upkeep than other materials.

Let's Talk Features

The details make all the difference. Whether you're designing from scratch or upgrading an existing pool, these ideas can inspire:

  • Lighting Magic: LED lights can transform your pool at night, creating an ambiance that's both inviting and safe.
  • Water Wonders: Add a touch of luxury with waterfalls, fountains, or even a playful splash pad for kids.
  • Deck Details: Surround your pool with stylish decking materials like travertine, concrete, or wood. Enhance comfort with built-in seating or a poolside bar.
  • Eco-Friendly Options: Solar heaters and variable-speed pumps not only save energy but also reduce costs.

Pool Maintenance Tips

Maintaining your pool ensures its longevity and keeps it looking its best. Here are a few pool maintenance tips:

  • Clean your pool regularly to prevent debris from collecting.
  • Check your pool's pH and chemical levels to keep the water safe and clear.
  • Keep your pool filter in good working condition for efficient circulation.

Wall & Step Brushing

Our technicians brush all pool walls, steps, benches, and tile lines to prevent algae from taking hold and to remove calcium and mineral buildup. Brushing is one of the most important — and most overlooked — parts of pool maintenance. Regular brushing keeps surfaces smooth, clean, and free of discoloration or staining.

Pool Vacuuming

We vacuum the entire pool floor and walls to remove settled dirt, sand, and fine debris that your filter can't catch on its own. A thorough vacuuming each visit keeps your pool floor spotless and prevents buildup that can stain surfaces over time. Combined with brushing and skimming, vacuuming completes the full cleaning process.

Skimmer & Pump Basket Cleaning

We empty and clean out your skimmer baskets and pump strainer basket every visit. Clogged baskets restrict water flow, reduce filtration efficiency, and can cause your pump to overheat or lose prime. Keeping these baskets clear ensures your entire circulation system runs smoothly and efficiently.

Equipment Inspection

During every service visit, we visually inspect your pool equipment — pump, motor, filter, heater, salt cell, and automation systems — to catch small problems before they become expensive repairs. We check for leaks, unusual noises, pressure changes, and signs of wear. Early detection saves you money and keeps your pool running without interruption.

Filter Cleaning

Filter cleaning is essential to maintaining proper water flow and filtration. Over time, filters accumulate oils, minerals, and debris that regular backwashing can't remove. We provide thorough filter disassembly, cleaning, and inspection as a separate service to keep your filtration system operating at peak performance. Filter cleaning frequency depends on your filter type and pool usage.
